# Veilborn — Project Vision & Build Strategy

> The north star. What we're making, how the pieces fit, and the order we build
> them. Everything else (the lore bible, the rules, the app) serves this.

---

## 1. What we're making

A **system-agnostic tabletop space-fantasy campaign setting** — usable with most
modern rulesets — that is three things at once:

1. A **ready-to-run authored galaxy** (deep lore, factions, a living metaplot).
2. A **toolkit / blank canvas** for DMs who'd rather build *their own* galaxy.
3. The home of two **signature subsystems** that make it unmistakably *this*
   setting: **Exploration & Cartography**, and **Ship Combat & Building**.

The product wins if a table can pick it up with any rules they already own and
have a blast **exploring, fighting in their ship, and getting tangled in faction
schemes** — or rip out the map and forge their own with our tools.

## 2. The four pillars (your vision, as guiding stars)

1. **Exploration & cartography are *fun*.** The map is the centerpiece. Charting
   the unknown should be a game in itself — risk, discovery, payoff, dread.
2. **A unique, engaging ship combat & building system.** Crew-driven, tactical,
   with shipbuilding that expresses identity. (Being redesigned from scratch.)
3. **A deep campaign *and* a framework.** Fully developed to play out of the box,
   *and* modular enough to be a blank canvas. Both, not either.
4. **Lore that's deep and fun.** Interlocking factions with clashing ambitions
   that force **hard choices**; a blend of **cosmic horror, wonder, mystery,
   action** (alien beasts, dogfights) and **humor**; living cultures and real
   history. The world should feel *alive*.

## 3. The core structural idea — three layers

The way to be *both* a finished setting and a blank canvas is to build in layers:

| Layer | What it is | Reusable? |
|---|---|---|
| **The Engine** | The universal subsystems — **Exploration/Cartography** & **Ship Combat/Building** — and the rules-agnostic glue. | Any galaxy, any ruleset. |
| **The Flagship** | **The Veilborn** itself: the 8 systems, worlds, cultures, factions, metaplot — fully authored, ready to run. | The worked example. |
| **The Forge** | The **DM toolkit**: generators, tables, and templates to build *your own* systems, worlds, and factions — using the Engine. | Build-your-own. |

Everything we author for the Flagship is *also* a demonstration of the Forge:
when we create a faction, we're implicitly defining the **faction template**; when
we chart Solara Prime, we're defining **how to chart a system**. Author once, learn
the pattern, ship the toolkit.

## 4. Tone & genre — the blend

The signature flavour is a **deliberate mix of registers**, and the mix is the
point: horror lands harder next to wonder; stakes hit harder next to humor.

- **Cosmic horror** — the unknowable, the wrong, the dread beneath the beauty.
- **Wonder & awe** — the galaxy is *gorgeous and vast*; exploration should make
  players gasp.
- **Mystery** — secrets, puzzles, things that don't add up; the map is full of
  question marks worth chasing.
- **Action** — alien beasts and horrors to fight; ship-to-ship dogfights.
- **Humor & heart** — banter, levity, found-family crew dynamics, room for great
  characters and arcs.

**Touchstones to triangulate (not copy):** Mass Effect, Firefly, Sunless
Skies/Sea, Dune, Hellboy-in-space, Disco Elysium's voice. **Rule of thumb:** never
all-horror or all-jokes for long — *alternate the registers*; let crews breathe,
then pull the floor out.

## 5. The faction web (the engine of "hard choices")

Great campaign lore = **factions whose ambitions can't all be satisfied**, orbiting
a **central mystery/threat**. Design approach:

- **4–6 major factions**, each with: an **Ambition**, **Methods**, **Assets/reach**,
  **Leadership**, a **Secret**, and **Relationships** to the others.
- A **central galactic mystery / slow apocalypse** (the cosmic-horror core) that
  every faction interprets and exploits differently — the thing the campaign
  ultimately turns on.
- **Hard-choice design:** helping one faction *costs* another; most "right answers"
  have a price. Track standing with a light **relationship/clock** system so player
  choices visibly ripple.
- Each faction answers: *why would the crew work with us?* and *what will we
  eventually ask them to choose?*
- This becomes the reusable **Faction Template** in the Forge.

## 6. Exploration & cartography — the spine

The map isn't set dressing; it's the **core loop**. We design exploration as a
game:

- **The discovery loop:** chart → encounter the unknown → risk/choice → reward &
  revelation → the map grows (and so does the danger).
- **The unknown is content:** every system holds a **mystery hook**, a **danger**,
  and a **prize**. Fog-of-war, rumors, and partial information drive the hunt.
- **Cartography as reward:** mapping, naming, and claiming are mechanically and
  narratively satisfying (the existing 3D map app is the table's shared chart).
- The Forge ships **system/world generators** so DMs can grow the unknown forever.

## 7. Making it feel *alive*

Beyond plot: the texture that sells a living galaxy.
- **Cultures** — distinct peoples, beliefs, aesthetics, ways of speaking.
- **History & timeline** — a real past with consequences in the present.
- **Bestiary** — alien beasts and cosmic horrors to encounter and fight.
- **Everyday detail** — food, trade, slang, music, what a dockworker complains
  about. Small things make big worlds believable.

## 8. System-agnostic & blank-canvas methodology

- **Rules-light by default.** Lore and exploration carry no system-specific math;
  the two subsystems use the host system's stats where possible and give short
  **conversion notes** (D&D, PbtA, d20, FATE, etc.).
- **Modular by design.** Every piece (a faction, a system, a beast, even the whole
  metaplot) is a **swappable module** with clear seams, so a DM can drop, replace,
  or reskin any of it without breaking the rest.
- **Two reading paths:** "Run the Veilborn as written" vs "Build your own with the
  Forge." The book signposts both.

## 9. Build order — two tracks, phased

Two parallel tracks feed the setting:

- **World Track** (lore/setting) — what this document mostly seeds.
- **Rules Track** (subsystems) — Exploration rules + the Ship Combat/Building
  redesign.

**Phases:**

- **Phase 0 — This plan.** ✅ (north star locked)
- **Phase 1 — The Galaxy Frame (light, top-down).** Just enough shared canvas so
  systems cohere: the **premise**, the **cosmology/how space & magic work**, the
  **tone pillars**, the **central mystery**, and the **factions-at-a-glance** (one
  line each). *Deliberately thin — details emerge from play.*
- **Phase 2 — System-by-system authoring (bottom-up).** Start with **Solara Prime**,
  then outward. Each system delivers: overview, worlds (culture/history/hooks),
  local faction presence, a beast/threat, a mystery, and adventure seeds. Refine
  the Frame as we learn.
- **Phase 3 — Weave the web.** Connect faction ambitions and the metaplot across
  systems; define the campaign arcs and the big hard-choice nodes.
- **Phase 4 — The Forge.** Extract generators/templates from how we built the
  Flagship (system, world, faction, beast, mystery generators).
- **Rules Track (in parallel):** redesign **Ship Combat & Building**, and design
  **Exploration & Cartography** rules — both as universal Engine modules.
- **Phase 5 — Production.** Art direction, layout, the companion app's role,
  playtest, commercialization.

> **On "start with Solara Prime":** we do — but we lay a *thin* Galaxy Frame first
> (Phase 1) so the first system has a sky to hang in. The Frame stays loose and
> grows with each system.

afterward shares a sky.

Then we go to **Solara Prime** and build outward.
