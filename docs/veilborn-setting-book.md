## How to use this book

The Veilborn is built to be played **two ways**:

- **Run it as written.** A ready galaxy — eight systems, living factions, and a slow
  apocalypse already ticking. Drop your crew in and go.
- **Build your own.** Treat every faction, world, and mystery as a **module** with clear
  seams. Keep what you like, reskin the rest, and use the toolkit (the *Forge*) to grow
  your own dark between the stars.

This book uses no system-specific math. Lore and exploration carry the story; the two
signature subsystems — **Exploration & Cartography** and **Ship Combat & Building** —
plug into your table's own rules with short conversion notes *(in development)*.

> 🔒 **A warning before you read on.** **Part IV is for the Gamemaster only.** The
> Veilborn has a public face and a hidden truth, and the campaign lives in the gap
> between them. Players: stop at the end of Part III. GMs: the real book starts at Part IV.

# PART I · THE GALAXY

🎨 [ Chapter-opener spread: the spiral of the Veilborn, eight systems pinned in light against the dark ]

## 1 · Welcome to the Veilborn

> ❝ *You were born under a sun. So was everyone you've ever loved. Out here, between
> them, is the dark we don't talk about — the dark the engines drink, the dark the
> mystics borrow from, the dark with a shape at the far edge of every chart that no
> captain will name aloud. We call it the Veil. We do not ask what's behind it.* ❞
> — a Charterhand's first lesson

The **Veilborn Galaxy** is a place of gorgeous, dangerous distances: gleaming
trade-cities and temple-foundries, alien beasts and uncharted deeps, dogfights in the
black and quiet horrors at the rim. It runs on one strange fact — that everything,
everywhere, floats on an ocean of **eternal darkness** — and on one great lie that
nobody alive remembers telling.

This book gives you the galaxy as its people understand it. What's *actually* true is
the GM's to reveal.

---

## 2 · The Veil & the Veilspinner

The **Veil** is the eternal darkness that surrounds everything — the void between and
behind the stars. It is not empty: **behind it lies something incomprehensible.** The
Veil is the wellspring of magic, of travel, and of *the weird.*

- **The Veilspinner** — the arcane engine in every ship. It **spins fragments of the
  Veil into reality** to fuel flight and warp travel.
- **Warp Skip** — to cross between systems, a ship **slips through the Veil** to a set of
  coordinates and re-emerges elsewhere. Deeply **power-hungry**; never casual. Whoever
  controls the charts and the fuel controls the galaxy.

> 📦 **SIDEBAR · Why the map matters.** Skip-coordinates are property; aether is a
> commodity; the dark is lethal to the unprepared. To explore is to gamble — or to pay
> the Chain. Charting the unknown is the campaign's core loop: *chart → meet the unknown
> → risk → reward & revelation → the map (and the danger) grows.*

---

## 3 · Magic

Magic in the Veilborn is **mysteriously fuelled by the Veil**, and kept deliberately
**ambiguous** so it rides atop *any* tabletop's magic rules. Casters draw on the Veil;
your table's own system says how it works. Where the power *comes from* — and what it
costs at the cosmic scale — is a question almost no one thinks to ask.

> 🔒 **GM'S EYES ONLY.** What the galaxy does **not** know: what the Veil really hides,
> what the stars truly are, or what the dark thing at the map's edge is calling for.
> All of that is **Part IV.**

# PART II · POWERS OF THE VEILBORN

> ❝ *Three powers run the lit galaxy: the ones who sell you the road, the ones who drew
> it, and the ones who pray over it. Cross all three and the dark is the safest place
> left for you.* ❞

## 5 · The Aureate Chain *(the trade hegemon)*

To most of the Veilborn, the **Aureate Chain** simply *is* civilisation — *"and
civilisation has a price the Chain sets."* It is the galaxy-spanning chartered trade
power, seated at the **City of Pearls** (Solara Prime), with its heartland in **Twin
Embers**.

**Its true power is the means of travel.** Warp Skip is power-hungry and the void is
lethal, so whoever controls **charts and fuel** controls everything:
- **The Charts.** The Survey maps; the Chain *licenses* the maps. Fly uncharted, gamble
  your ship.
- **The Fuel.** Refined **aether** flows through Chain depots. Cut off, you don't fly.

This makes the Chain the natural **patron *and* antagonist** of any crew that wants to
explore. *You can go anywhere in the galaxy — as long as the Chain sells you the map and
the fuel to get there.*

**Structure.** **Chain-Lords** (regional sovereigns) · **Counting Houses** (the
bureaucracy) · the **Ironbound** (the "we have no military" military) · **Factors**
(agents and fixers) · **Charterhands** (chartered captains — *where most crews sit*).

**Leadership.**
- **Chain-Lord Lotus Silverblood** *(City of Pearls)* — the honest public sovereign; he
  believes the Chain is exactly what it appears.
- **Chain-Lady Mirreth** *(the Ironbound, Twin Embers)* — the iron fist of the Twins;
  commands the fleets and the foundries.

> 🛠️ **AT THE TABLE.** Most crews owe the Chain money, charters, or both. It's the hand
> that feeds and the hand that squeezes.
> 🔒 *What the Chain really is: Part IV, Ch. 14.*

---

## 6 · The Arcane Survey *(cartographers & keepers of knowledge)*

If the Chain sells the road, the **Arcane Survey** *drew* it. Born as Twin Embers
scholars and cartographers, it is now the galaxy's **mapping authority** and its great
**library** — of systems, worlds, and moons, and sometimes of secrets and politics
better left unfiled.

- **Clearance** runs **Tier 1–5**; the deepest vaults (**Tier 5**) hold what the public
  must never read.
- **Stations** stand in the safe systems; a few sit **abandoned** at the galaxy's
  feared edges; none at all in the Crimson Maw.

The Survey is the crew's likeliest source of leads, lore, and licensed exploration work
— and a place where curiosity has a way of becoming dangerous.

> 🔒 *The Survey has a corruption, a hidden cell, and a buried mystery. Part IV, Ch. 14.*

---

## 7 · The Faith of the Stargiver *(the great religion)*

The dominant religion of the Veilborn, and on Twin Embers the state, the guild, and the
soul of the economy at once.

> ❝ *He came down from the dark and gave us the stars. Then he died to keep a horror
> from being born. Grieve him — and never sail where he forbade.* ❞ — common catechism

**What the faithful believe.** Roughly three and a half centuries ago an **alien**
descended on Twin Embers — then a Renaissance world — and in **69 years** lifted it to
the computer age and the stars. He is the **Stargiver**, the messiah who gave humanity
the heavens. Then, the faithful hold, he **gave his life** to stop a cosmic horror, and
ascended into legend. Theirs is a **genuine, joyful, grieving, beloved faith.**

**The silver serpent.** The faith's symbol — a coiled or descending silver serpent,
worn as a **pendant** at the throat of dockworkers and Chain-Lords alike, etched over
foundry doors, pressed into coins across the Veilborn.

**The clergy.** Lampbearers (parish) → Foundry-Fathers & Mothers (the temple-foundries)
→ Radiants (regional) → **the First Light** (the church's supreme, sincere head).

**Holy days.** The **Coming** (his descent); the **Quickening** (the 69-year uplift — a
festival season of invention and light); the **Ascension** (the solemn day of mourning,
when the **Last Transmission** is read and his final command renewed).

**The prohibition.** His dying word forbade the faithful to follow him to the
**Voidmother**. To this day, ordinary folk shun that system as holy ground and a grave;
pilots pray not to drift toward it. To sail there is not merely dangerous — it is
**heresy against the martyr's wish.**

> 🔒 **GM'S EYES ONLY.** Every word of the founding story is a lie, and the prohibition
> is a crime scene sealed with the love of a galaxy. See Part IV, Ch. 12 & 14.

# PART IV · THE HIDDEN TRUTH

> 🔒 **GM'S EYES ONLY — STOP HERE IF YOU ARE A PLAYER.**
>
> Everything that follows is the secret the whole galaxy is built on top of without
> knowing. Deliver it like a thunderclap: the truth that recontextualises every sunrise.

🎨 [ Ominous chapter-opener: a serpent coiled around a dying star ]

## 10 · The Star-Mother & the Hungry Void

**The Voidmother is not a monster. She is where the light comes from.** The being the
galaxy fears is a **star-mother** — a cosmic entity whose nature is the *creation of
suns*. She carries each new star from a **gestating egg** to a burning body, guided
every step by her own **arcane resonance**.

> **The central truth.** A child guided in her resonance becomes **a star.** A child
> that hatches *without* her becomes its inversion — light turned inside out, a sun that
> consumes instead of gives: **a black hole.**

**The Hearthstone.** Her gestating eggs are called **Hearthstones** — a star's heart in
its cradle, held in her **womb** (her region of resonance). A Hearthstone is a thing of
miracles: **limitless power**, and a force that **remakes matter, heals any wound, cures
any sickness, and reverses ageing.** Ripened in her resonance it becomes a sun. Torn out
and drained, it **curdles** — and, in time, hatches into a **Devourer.**

**The Devourer** *(VB-005)*, then, is no accident of the cosmos. It is a **stolen
star-heart, drained and abandoned until it curdled** — and the star-mother *knows* the
black hole at the galaxy's edge is her stolen child. She has grieved it across an age,
dormant in her region **(VB-008)**.

> 📦 **THE CHORUS.** The Devourer's thousands of voices — everything it has consumed —
> all say one word, each heard in its own tongue: ***please.*** A sun's worth of
> yearning, calling for the only force that could have made it whole.

## 11 · The First People *(the Keth'aal)*

Long before Twin Embers lit its first lamp, the galaxy's first great civilisation was
the **Keth'aal**: a **silver-scaled, reptilian, deeply psionic** people, **200–250 years
to a life**, who thought in deep time. Their oldest scripture was the **finding of the
star-mother** — and that worship curdled into theft.

They breached her womb and took a Hearthstone. For **millennia** they ran a golden age on
a stolen star-heart and its miracles, hiding it from its mother — until it **curdled.**
One ultra-rich house, the **Aurun Line**, foresaw the end and **fled with a fleet of
riches**; the rest stayed and were **swallowed** as the Hearthstone hatched into the
**Devourer** that ate their homeworld, **Sael'aan** — *"the silver cradle."*

> **Their sin in one line:** *they found god, bled her child for an age to light their
> empire, and forged the hunger that devoured them.*

**The public face.** The galaxy knows them only as *"the First People"* — a vanished
precursor race of silver ruins and psionic relics the Survey covets. **No one connects
them to the Devourer.** A romantic archaeological mystery — not the answer to the cosmic
horror at the edge of the map.

---

## 12 · The Stargiver *(Iss'reth Aurun)*

The last living Keth'aal, sole scion of the **Aurun Line**, and the **central
antagonist** — the immortal who built the modern galaxy, hid its original sin, and is
quietly steering it toward a second.

| Face | Who holds it | What they see |
|---|---|---|
| **The martyred saviour** | the galaxy / the Faith 🔓 | The alien who gave the stars, then died a martyr — mourned every Ascension. |
| **The living guide** | the Silver Coil 🔒 | He *still lives*, and directs them. They kill to keep it secret. |
| **Iss'reth Aurun** | him alone 🔒 | The Keth'aal who stole a star, murdered to keep it, and means to master what killed his world. |

**The charming predator.** Magnetic, gracious, genuinely brilliant — the most likeable
person in any room, and he made the room. The uplift of Twin Embers was *real*, but the
good was always **instrumental**: it bought him the Chain, the Survey, an industrial
base, and adoration. He treats people as pieces, and delights in the long game.

**The fatal certainty.** He **knows** his second stolen Hearthstone is curdling toward a
new Devourer — and is serenely sure he will **master it** where his "careless" ancestors
failed. His blind spot: *only the star-mother's resonance can ripen an egg; there is no
substitute.* He is Sael'aan happening again, narrated by a man certain it's a different
story.

**The hidden leash.** The Hearthstone made him deathless, but he must periodically
return to its resonance (the City of Pearls) or the lost centuries catch him. This roots
him, gives a clock — and seals the central trap: **the only way to stop the Devourer is
to return the stone to its mother, which kills him.** His life and the galaxy's doom are
the same object.

> **The Ascension — the founding lie.** At 222 YA he led the Survey's best to the womb,
> **murdered them all**, took the Hearthstone, and broadcast the **Last Transmission** —
> recasting the massacre as heroic martyrdom (*"we died stopping a cosmic horror; never
> go to the Voidmother"*). So he "died," ascended to legend, and vanished into hidden
> rule before anyone could mark that he no longer ages. **He didn't stop a cosmic horror
> — he stole the seed of one. The martyrs are his victims.**

> 🛠️ **AT THE TABLE.** Meet him before you know him — a charming benefactor in disguise.
> His defeat *is* his death, which he'll never accept, so he can't be reasoned into
> surrender, only out-maneuvered. The one place his composure fails: the **Devourer**,
> where the Chorus carries his own people, still saying *please.*

## 13 · The Silver Coil & the Vesh'kaar

Beneath the beloved public faith runs the **Silver Coil** — the Stargiver's cult of
assassins, named for the serpent: the coil you never see, wound through everything. Most
of the faithful never know it exists.

**What it guards.** The Coil's secret is that the Stargiver **still lives.** What it does
*not* know is that, in hiding his survival, it hides **something far worse** — the theft
and slow collapse of an unborn star. The cult guards an apocalypse it cannot comprehend.

**Structure.** The Faithful → the Called → the **Coiled** (sworn agents) → the
**Vesh'kaar** (the seven) → the Stargiver.

### The Vesh'kaar — the Seven Fangs

The Stargiver's seven master assassins, ranked **1–7** — *Vesh'kaar* is his own dead
tongue: **"the Fangs."** Each is unique; together they form an escalating ladder (the
crew meets the higher numbers first, the **First** last). Every Vesh knows the Stargiver
lives and that his power flows from a **relic of "veil-magic"** — but no more than that.

- **Vesh #1 — *the First*.** Leader, spymaster, deadliest. A **faceless spider** with no
  name or face, existing only as **personas** the crew may already trust. Master of
  combat, subterfuge, and infiltration alike; armed with the Chain's best tech via #4.
  **Bound by debt and love** to the Stargiver, who found him ruined and remade him — the
  one human thing left in him is devotion to a monster. *Crack:* a "tell" that persists
  across all his faces.
- **Vesh #4 — *Chain-Lady Mirreth*.** The most public Fang: the **iron fist of Twin
  Embers**, commanding the Ironbound and the galaxy's best war-foundries — force at every
  scale, from fleet to her own blade. A **true zealot**, righteous and deceived; she
  shields Lotus Silverblood as perfect cover and has no idea she guards an apocalypse.
  *Crack:* the truth she's never been trusted with would shatter her.
- **Vesh #2, #3, #5, #6** — *open.* · **Vesh #7** — the newest and least proven; the face
  the crew likely meets first.

## 14 · The True Faces *(what the powers really are)*

- **The Aureate Chain** is the Stargiver's apparatus — founded on the Aurun Line's fled
  fortune (the very name **Aur**eate is the buried **Aur**un name), now guarding his
  stolen Hearthstone and his rule. Steered from within by the Silver Coil. *Lotus is the
  honest face atop a lie he's never seen.*
- **The Arcane Survey** is a battleground — most of it sincere scholarship, but riddled
  with the Coil's **"rot,"** and home to a **hidden cell** quietly investigating the
  founding expedition (a thread that leads straight to the truth).
- **The Faith of the Stargiver** is the ocean the Coil swims in: a real, good, beloved
  religion whose holiest sorrow mourns a death that never happened, and whose dearest
  command keeps the galaxy away from the scene of the crime.

> 🛠️ **THE CRUELLEST HARD CHOICE.** The faith is *real and good for people.* To expose
> the truth is to shatter a beloved church, topple the galaxy's industrial heart, and
> brand its martyr a murderer. *Do you tear down three centuries of light to reveal the
> dark beneath it?*

---

## 15 · Running the Reckoning *(the metaplot)*

After **222 years** out of resonance, the stolen Hearthstone nears its hatching. The
**Reckoning** is the star-mother — twice robbed — stirring at last to reclaim her second
child before it curdles into a Devourer inside the most populated system in the galaxy.

- **The clock.** Left unchecked, the City of Pearls becomes the cradle of a second
  Devourer. The Stargiver swears he'll master it; he won't.
- **The only cure kills the villain.** The stone must go home to its mother. That ends
  the second Devourer — and the Stargiver with it.
- **Who can be turned.** Lotus (if woken), Mirreth (if the truth could reach her), the
  Survey's hidden cell, even — at the edge of possibility — the grieving star-mother
  herself, if someone learns and **speaks her true name**: *the one who lights the dark.*

> **Player-initiated.** The Reckoning's stages unfold from the moment the crew triggers
> it — measured in relative time from there. The galaxy, sensing the egg come into play,
> begins to answer.
